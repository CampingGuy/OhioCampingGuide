# 1. Introduction & How to Use This Guide

This guide consolidates every known free, primitive, dispersed, and backpack camping location in Ohio and immediately adjacent Western Pennsylvania. It is intended to serve as a single field manual for:

- Weekend car campers
- Backpackers
- Hunters and anglers
- ATV/OHV riders
- Hikers
- Long-term travelers seeking low-cost stays

Every campground entry uses a **standardized format** :

- **Address** - **GPS** (decimal degrees; DMS included for select sites)
- **Elevation** - **Management** - **Overview** - **Camping Rules** - **Amenities** - **Activities** - **Nearby Supplies** - **Nearby Emergency Resources** - **Cell Service** - **Road Access / Difficulty** - **Best Season** - **Notes**  **Always verify information before traveling.** State and federal agencies periodically change rules, close roads, or alter camping availability. Confirm with the managing agency.

# 2. Ohio Camping Laws & Regulations

## Wayne National Forest (USFS)

Dispersed camping is permitted on most National Forest land unless posted otherwise.

- 14 consecutive day maximum stay
- Camp 300+ feet from developed recreation areas where practical
- Pack out all trash
- Follow posted fire restrictions
- Verify public land boundaries (private inholdings exist)
 **Official resource:** Wayne National Forest Headquarters, 13700 US Hwy 33, Nelsonville, OH | (740) 753-0101

## Jesse Owens State Park (ODNR)

- Camping only permitted in designated campgrounds
- Self-registration at campground kiosks
- No camping fee
- 14-day maximum stay

## Ohio State Forests (ODNR Division of Forestry)

- Camping permitted only in designated campgrounds or designated backpack camps
- Roadside dispersed camping is generally prohibited
- Firewood rules apply (no out-of-state firewood)

## State Wildlife Areas (ODNR Division of Wildlife)

- Camping is generally prohibited unless specifically posted
- Some allow primitive camping during hunting seasons

## Private Land

Camping without landowner permission is illegal. Always confirm public land status via:
- County GIS parcel maps (free online)
- USFS Motor Vehicle Use Maps (MVUM)
- ODNR property atlases
- onX Offroad or Gaia GPS (paid apps with ownership overlays)

# 3. Leave No Trace Principles

1. Plan ahead and prepare
2. Travel and camp on durable surfaces
3. Dispose of waste properly (pack it out)
4. Leave what you find
5. Minimize campfire impacts (use existing fire rings)
6. Respect wildlife
7. Be considerate of other visitors

---

# 4. General Camping Safety

- Always carry a paper map and compass as backup
- Download offline maps before entering remote areas
- Treat all natural water sources
- Check weather forecasts (NOAA Weather Radio recommended)
- Inform someone of your itinerary
- Carry a first-aid kit
- Bear bags/food storage is good practice
- Check for ticks after hiking

## 7.1 Wildcat Hollow Trail & Dispersed Camping
 **Address:** Co Rd 58, Glouster, OH 45732   **GPS:** 39.5740, -82.0664   **DMS:** 39°34'26.4"N, 82°03'59.0"W   **Elevation:** ~860 ft   **Management:** Wayne National Forest

### Overview
Wildcat Hollow is Ohio's premier backpacking destination, offering two main loops: a 17.2-mile loop with ridge-top and stream-bottom sections, and a 5-mile loop for shorter hikes. Terrain includes tall pine forests, open meadows, quiet streams, rock outcroppings, and old forest roads with scenic overlooks.

### Camping Rules
- Dispersed camping permitted at trailhead and along trail
- 14-day maximum stay
- Park only on gravel surfaces
- Pack out all trash

### Amenities
- None (primitive)
- No toilets, no potable water

### Activities
- Backpacking, day hiking, wildlife viewing, photography
- Spring wildflower viewing (peak April–May)
- Fall foliage (peak October)

### Nearby Supplies
- **J J's Market:** 117 E Main St, Corning, OH
- **John's Place:** 105 11th St, Corning, OH
- **Altheirs Oil / Sunoco:** W Main St, Corning, OH
- **Kroger:** 240 W Main St, McConnelsville, OH (~12.5 miles)

### Nearby Emergency Resources
- **Nearest Hospital:** Hocking Valley Community Hospital, Logan (~25 miles)
- **Ranger Station:** Athens Ranger District, Nelsonville (~15 miles)
- **Sheriff:** Athens County Sheriff, (740) 593-6600

### Cell Service
- Verizon: Limited
- AT&T: Spotty
- T-Mobile: Poor

### Road Access
Passenger car accessible in dry conditions. Avoid after heavy rain (unpaved road becomes muddy).

### Best Season
April–May (wildflowers), October (fall colors)

### Notes
- One of the quietest dispersed camping areas in Ohio
- Clockwise navigation recommended
- Wildlife includes white-tailed deer, wild turkey, occasional black bear sightings

## 7.3 Hanging Rock OHV & Ponds
 **Address:** Park Road 105, Ironton, OH   **GPS:** 38.5807, -82.7242   **Management:** Wayne National Forest | **Phone:** (740) 534-6500

### Overview
Multi-use recreation area with ATV/OHV trails and 51 fishing ponds. Primitive camping permitted at trailheads and ponds during the OHV season.

### Camping Rules
- Open April 14 – December 14 (OHV season)
- 14-day maximum stay
- First-come, dispersed

### Amenities
- Vault toilets (selected areas)
- Fishing ponds
- Large open areas for tents and RVs (no hookups)

### Activities
- ATV/OHV riding, fishing, boating (limited), hunting, mountain biking (seasonal), wildlife viewing

### Permits (OHV Use)
- $12/day
- $24 / 3-day
- $45 / season

### Directions
From US-52, take OH-650 north to Forest Road 105; continue ~1.5 miles.

### Cell Service
Verizon: Fair | AT&T: Limited | T-Mobile: Poor

### Road Access
High-clearance recommended; some trail spurs are rugged

### Notes
Terrain includes reclaimed strip-mined land with steep slopes

# 9. Muskingum River Lock Campgrounds

The Muskingum River Navigation System features 10 hand-operated locks. Several allow public camping.

## 9.1 Luke Chute (Lock #5)
 **Address:** Waterford, OH   **GPS:** 39.5380, -81.7200   **Elevation:** 656 ft   **Management:** ODNR – Watercraft

### Overview
Free riverside camping along the Muskingum River at Lock & Dam #5. One of the few locks allowing public camping.

### Camping Rules
- 14-day maximum stay
- First-come, first-served
- Open year-round

### Amenities
- Outhouses, drinking fountains, open tent areas, small RV space (no hookups), river access

### Activities
- Fishing, wildlife viewing, river recreation

### Notes
- Firewood often available locally (delivered by residents for small fee)
- Historical marker on-site
- Closest town: Waterford (~5 miles); Beverly, OH across river

# 17. Decision Support: Which Site Should I Choose?

## By Weather
- **Hot summer:** Jesse Owens (lake access), Luke Chute (river)
- **Cold/winter:** Jesse Owens (shorter access), Round Bottom PA
- **Rainy:** Jesse Owens (vault toilets), Harrison State Forest (paved)

## By Experience Level
- **Beginner:** Jesse Owens (Hook Lake, Sand Hollow), Round Bottom PA
- **Intermediate:** Wildcat Hollow short loop, Lake Vesuvius
- **Advanced:** Wildcat Hollow long loop, Shawnee Backpack Trail

## By Solitude
- **Most quiet:** Wildcat Hollow, Maple Grove, Vinton Furnace
- **Moderate:** Jesse Owens weekday, Stone Church
- **Least quiet:** Hanging Rock (OHV weekends), Hook Lake (summer weekends)

## By Vehicle Type
- **RV-friendly:** Jesse Owens (Hook Lake, Sand Hollow, Maple Grove), Harrison State Forest, Round Bottom PA
- **Small camper OK:** Most Jesse Owens sites, Hanging Rock
- **Tent only:** Wildcat Hollow, Monday Creek trailheads, backpack camps

## By Season
- **Best spring:** Wildcat Hollow (wildflowers), Jesse Owens
- **Best summer:** Jesse Owens (lake), Hanging Rock (ponds), Luke Chute
- **Best fall:** Wildcat Hollow (foliage), Shawnee State Forest
- **Best winter:** Jesse Owens (less crowded), Round Bottom PA
