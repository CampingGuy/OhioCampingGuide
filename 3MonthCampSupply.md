# 3-Month Vehicle Camping Plan

### Stone Church Trailhead – Wayne National Forest, Ohio

---

## Overview

This guide is intended for a long-term vehicle camping trip where you have a vehicle for storage but may not have a camping stove or reliable opportunities to resupply. It emphasizes practical planning, food storage, hydration, safety, and comfort.

---

# Important Rules

* Dispersed camping in Wayne National Forest is generally limited to **14 consecutive days** at one location.
* Plan to move to another permitted dispersed camping area before reaching the stay limit.
* Always follow current U.S. Forest Service regulations and local fire restrictions.

# Water

Bring enough drinking water to last between refill opportunities.

Recommended equipment:

* Three or four 5-gallon water containers
* Water filter
* Water purification tablets

**Do not drink untreated creek water.**

---

# Vehicle Use

Your vehicle should serve as:

* Food storage
* Gear storage
* Emergency shelter during storms

Store all food inside the vehicle.

Never keep food inside your tent.

---

# Sleeping

Because the vehicle has no air conditioning, sleeping inside during an Ohio summer can become dangerously hot.

Recommended setup:

* Sleep in a tent.
* Choose a shaded campsite whenever possible.
* Keep tent vents open for airflow.
* Install a tarp over the tent during rain.

---

# Heat Safety

Summer temperatures can reach:

* 90–96°F (32–36°C)

Recommendations:

* Drink water frequently.
* Stay in the shade during the hottest part of the day.
* Wear lightweight clothing.
* Limit strenuous activity during peak afternoon heat.

---

# Rain

Bring:

* Heavy-duty tarp
* Extra stakes
* Paracord
* Waterproof storage bins
* Rain jacket

Expect muddy conditions after heavy rainfall.

---

# Insects

Expect:

* Mosquitoes
* Ticks
* Chiggers

Bring:

* Insect repellent
* Long pants
* Long sleeves
* Tweezers or a tick-removal tool

Perform a tick check every evening.

---

# Wildlife

Possible wildlife includes:

* Raccoons
* Mice
* Black bears (less common but present)

Keep food secured inside the vehicle.

Dispose of trash regularly.

Keep your campsite clean.

# Safety Reminders

* Never sleep inside a sealed vehicle during hot weather.
* Stay hydrated every day.
* Store food securely inside your vehicle.
* Watch for signs of heat illness such as dizziness, confusion, or nausea.
* Keep a backup plan in case severe weather or another emergency requires leaving the area.
